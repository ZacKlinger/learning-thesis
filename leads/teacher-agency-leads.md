# Leads — teacher agency

Unverified material relevant to the teacher-agency thread. Nothing here has been read by the agent. Promotion to `claim-evidence/` requires a verbatim quote + URL + access date.

## Primary sources to read

- **Apple, Michael W. (1995). *Education and Power* (2nd ed.).** Frequently cited canonical text on de-skilling of teachers and the politics of curriculum. Need primary text, not the secondary literature on it.
- **Au, Wayne (2011). "Teaching Under the New Taylorism: High-Stakes Testing and the Standardization of the 21st Century Curriculum."** *Journal of Curriculum Studies*, 43(1). Direct empirical engagement with the de-skilling claim under NCLB-era accountability.
- **Cuban, Larry (1993). *How Teachers Taught: Constancy and Change in American Classrooms, 1890–1990*.** Historical evidence on how persistent teacher practice has been across reforms — relevant in both directions (teachers absorb/resist reform).
- **Tyack, David & Cuban, Larry (1995). *Tinkering Toward Utopia: A Century of Public School Reform*.** The historical case against grand-reform narratives. Useful for not over-claiming "schools used to be different."
- **Lemov, Doug (2010, with editions through 2021). *Teach Like a Champion*.** The most influential articulation of the structured-teaching tradition. Required steelman.
- **Engelmann, Siegfried. *Direct Instruction* writings.** The most aggressive scripted-curriculum tradition. Project Follow Through results are part of the canon to reckon with.
- **Mehta, Jal (2013). *The Allure of Order: High Hopes, Dashed Expectations, and the Troubled Quest to Remake American Schooling*.** History of the technocratic reform impulse and its costs.

## Empirical work to locate

- Project Follow Through (1968–1995). The largest US educational evaluation ever conducted; widely cited as showing Direct Instruction outperformed alternatives. The validity of that summary is contested — read the primary reports, not the slogans.
- Meta-analyses of scripted vs. teacher-designed curricula on student outcomes — does one exist with adequate methodological care?
- Research on novice vs. expert teachers' use of scripted materials (the "floor effect" hypothesis).
- Studies of teacher turnover correlated with curriculum scripting / autonomy.

## Practitioner / commentary leads

- Diane Ravitch, *The Death and Life of the Great American School System* (2010) — insider recantation on accountability-era reforms.
- Bryk, Sebring, Allensworth, Luppescu & Easton (2010), *Organizing Schools for Improvement* (Chicago school reform study) — empirical work on the organizational conditions for teacher effectiveness.
- Roosevelt, *The Teacher Wars* (Goldstein 2014) — narrative history of the profession.

## Tensions to chase

- The de-skilling literature (Apple, Au) and the structured-teaching literature (Lemov, Engelmann) are largely talking past each other. A serious version of the thread needs to engage both, not just the one that flatters the thesis.
- "Teacher agency" as a value can be in tension with equity if low-agency-but-evidence-based curricula raise outcomes for the lowest-served students. This is the hardest counter-argument and the one Zack should write through, not around.
