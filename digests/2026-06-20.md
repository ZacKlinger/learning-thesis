- [Zeiser et al. 2014 — *Evidence of Deeper Learning Outcomes* (AIR/
  Hewlett Report 3)](../sources/Zeiser-et-al-2014-deeper-learning-outcomes.md) — quasi-experimental matched-pair evaluation of 10
  deeper-learning high school networks (~14,000 students). 0.10–0.20

  and low-prior-achievement students. Real and contemporary at-scale
  evidence, modest in magnitude, with honest authorial caveats about
  generalisation and between-pair variation.

## Tensions I noticed

The biggest tension this week is **unit-of-analysis**.

Three of the four sources read this Saturday make claims at *different
units of analysis* than the ones already on the ledger, and the
collision is methodologically interesting:

- KSC and SKC reason at the **individual cognitive level** — working
  memory, the worked-example effect, schema acquisition in long-term
  memory.
- HSDC and Scardamalia & Bereiter reason at the **knowledge-building
  community level** — discourse patterns, peer-review processes,
  collective artefacts.
- Bryk reasons at the **school-organisational level** — essential
  supports, leadership, climate, professional capacity.
- Zeiser reasons at the **school-network level** — what happens when 10
  networks of schools all pursue deeper-learning practices with
  moderate-or-better implementation fidelity.

None of these levels is the wrong level. But they are not
interchangeable, and the ledger has been treating them as though they
were. The thesis owes a position on *which unit of analysis matches
which question*. A defensible cut:

- For "does this pedagogy efficiently transfer declarative knowledge to
  novices?" → individual cognitive level (KSC's home turf, where his
  case is strongest).
- For "does this technology enable a kind of learning that was not
  previously possible?" → knowledge-building community level (S&B's
  home turf, where his case sits).
- For "does this school produce better outcomes than that school?" →
  school-organisational level (Bryk's home turf).
- For "would these effects scale beyond the schools that have already
  adopted the model?" → school-network level (Zeiser's home turf — and
  the answer his design *cannot* fully give, by his own admission).

Once the units are separated, the apparent disagreement between KSC
and Scardamalia & Bereiter narrows. They are not really contesting the
same question; they are contesting *which question matters*. The
methodological standoff is real, but it is a standoff over priorities,
not over evidence.

The second tension is smaller but specific. **The Bryk evidence
complicates the steer's workload-only framing.** The steer (2026-05-27)
frames AI's role in teacher agency through "the system-imposed workload
that forces teachers to teach to the middle of 30." Bryk's data shows
that workload-relief alone — without the other four essential supports
— does not produce school improvement. Schools weak on learning climate
plus instruction did not improve attendance even when other supports
were present. This is not a contradiction of the steer; it is a
sharpening. AI deployments that solve workload-relief without
engaging the other supports should not be expected to produce the
school-improvement effects Bryk's data records. The teacher-agency
thread is updated accordingly.

## Thesis-fit check

Current line from the 2026-05-27 steer (operative direction):

*"AI specifically removes four constraints: the cost of expert
attention; the one-way nature of fixed content; the system-imposed
workload that forces teachers to teach to the middle of 30; the gap
between students and expert-level practice."*

This week's reading **partially supports and partially complicates**
the four-constraint frame.

**Partial support.** Scardamalia & Bereiter operationalise a kind of
learning that depends on technology making it feasible at scale — the
knowledge-building community. This is the cleanest mechanism case the
augmentation aim has had. Their explicit contrast between
student-centred intentionality (CSILE) and system-centred
intentionality (intelligent tutoring) is a *positive specification* of
what makes the steer's aim different from the marketing version
Watters 2018 critiques. Zeiser provides modest at-scale outcome
evidence for structurally similar practices (PBL, collaboration,
portfolios) — 0.10–0.20 SD effects, equity across prior-achievement
levels, 9 percentage points on graduation.

**Partial complication.** The four-constraint frame has the four
constraints as parallel — each addressed independently by AI removing
the constraint. Bryk's data shows the school-improvement variables
are *not* independent: they operate jointly. SKC's sur-rejoinder
challenges the existence of any "general teachable problem-solving
strategy" — a direct challenge to the deeper-learning frame Zeiser's
mechanism story rests on. The honest read is that constraint-removal
in service of the steer's aim requires more than four-parallel-fixes;
it requires a constellation of supports that compound. The thesis owes
an account of how the four constraints relate to one another in the
absence-versus-presence of the broader essential-supports framework
Bryk's data describes.

**Empirical evidence status.** After this Saturday: constraint #1 (cost
of expert attention) still has no contemporary empirical anchor on the
ledger (VanLehn 2011 demoted; no secondary citations validated yet).
Constraint #2 (one-way fixed content) has Cuban 2025 (small
practitioner) and now Scardamalia & Bereiter (mechanism; the CSILE
community database vs. the textbook/lecture default). Constraint #3
(workload) has Cuban 2025 (small practitioner) and Bryk (structural-
conditions framework, with the workload-only sharpening recorded
above). Constraint #4 (gap to expert practice) has S&B's "second-order
environments" framing (mechanism) and is partially served by Zeiser
(deeper-learning network outcomes including selective-college
enrollment). So: two of four constraints now have both
mechanism + at-scale outcome evidence; one of four (workload) has
practitioner + structural-conditions evidence; one of four (cost of
expert attention) remains empirically thin pending VanLehn or a
substitute.
