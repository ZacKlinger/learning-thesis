## Tensions I noticed

Two worth surfacing. One inside the KSC/HSDC exchange; one between the
thesis's clarified frame and Cuban.

**Inside KSC/HSDC: both sides bound their own claim, and the productive
question is conditional.** The surface reading of "KSC vs. HSDC" is a
binary — direct guidance wins, or inquiry wins. The actual papers,
read against each other, end up in a much more interesting place. KSC
concede (p. 80) the expertise-reversal effect: their finding is bounded
to novices. HSDC concede (p. 106) that they do not yet know how to balance
scaffolded inquiry with direct guidance, and that the broader goals of
learning (epistemic practices, soft skills, self-directed learning) are
"not measured on achievement tests but are important." Each paper bounds
its own claim, in a different direction. The thesis can use this: there is
no general empirical winner; the productive questions are *conditional* on
learner state, domain, and outcome metric. That conditional framing is
exactly what the steer's positive thesis needs — "the truest forms of
learning, whatever the research says those forms actually are" — because
it asserts that the empirical question is real and conditional rather
than something the thesis has to pre-resolve.

**Between the thesis and Cuban: Cuban is harder to fold into the steer's
"medium evolves" framing than the steer assumes.** The steer reads
tech-cycles as setup: every prior medium changed something about
learning; AI is the next change. Cuban (in 2024, the most-recent and
AI-explicit statement of his view) says the opposite: every prior medium
*did not* change what teachers actually do, and AI won't either, because
edtech advocates fundamentally misunderstand the complexity of teaching.
The two framings are not compatible. The thesis can honor Cuban's
historical record while declining his prediction, but it has to be
honest about what it is doing. The most useful move is to take Cuban's
"complexity of teaching" claim *seriously* and treat it as a *constraint
on what good AI-in-education designs look like* — designs that ignore
the constraint deserve Cuban's critique; designs that respect it are
the place the thesis's positive case has to operate. The thread file
records this gap rather than papering over it.

## Thesis-fit check

Current line from the 2026-05-27 steer (the operative direction):
*"The optimistic case is not about a particular pedagogy. It is about
removing the constraints that have forced education to compromise on
its aim. AI specifically removes four constraints: the cost of expert
attention; the one-way nature of fixed content; the system-imposed
workload that forces teachers to teach to the middle of 30; the gap
between students and expert-level practice."*

This week's reading supports the frame in one specific way and
complicates it in another.

*Supports:* HSDC's strongest contribution — that productive PBL/IL
environments scaffold student work through expert modeling, embedded
guidance, just-in-time direct instruction, and tool-imposed structure
— is *precisely* what AI is now capable of providing at near-zero
marginal cost. The four-constraint frame predicts that AI's role is
to enable the kinds of scaffolded inquiry HSDC describe at scale and
quality previously available only in well-resourced research-school
settings (GenScope, Knowledge Forum, the Jasper Woodbury implementations).
The supporting sprint's first sources (Scardamalia & Bereiter; the
Deeper Learning evaluations) are squarely in this lineage.

*Complicates:* Cuban's "complexity of teaching" claim is a real
constraint on the *workload* leg of the four-constraint frame. The
steer says AI gives teachers back time and personalization-bandwidth.
Cuban says that what AI advocates think they are giving teachers — by
automating "the mechanical parts" of teaching — does not exist as a
clean separable thing. Teaching is improvisational, relational, and
emotional all the way down. The thesis's workload-relief argument has
to specify *what* AI is automating without crossing the line into
substituting for the relational core. That specification is real work
the thesis has not yet done.

No re-steer requested — both directions point at the supporting sprint.
