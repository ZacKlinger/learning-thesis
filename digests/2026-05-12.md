## Tensions I noticed

Two structural tensions surfaced just from picking the canon. Both will bite the thesis if you do not write through them.

1. **The strongest empirical evidence for educational technology is efficiency, not augmentation.** Bloom's 2-sigma, the cognitive-tutor literature, retrieval-practice apps, mastery-learning systems — these are the most-replicable wins. Your seed v1 sets up augmentation as the noble path and efficiency as the wrong one. But empirically, efficiency is what holds up under scrutiny. The augmentation literature (Logo, Knowledge Building, deeper-learning networks) is more aspirational and less robust. Either the thesis embraces some of the efficiency case as legitimate, or it owes us a high standard of proof for the augmentation case it does want to make.

2. **"Teacher agency" can sit in real tension with equity.** The honest version of the steelman: in under-resourced schools with high teacher turnover, scripted curricula (Direct Instruction, Lemov-style structured teaching, high-fidelity phonics) raise the floor in measurable, robust ways. A thesis that treats agency-removing reforms as a uniform category error will fail to engage the strongest counter-evidence. The harder argument — that AI/tech should support teacher judgment *without* defaulting to scripting — is the one worth making.
