# Thesis seeds

**Status:** v1 — strongly-felt threads as of 2026-05-11. Expected to evolve. The research agent should *not* harden these into fixed claims; surface tensions actively.

## Working frame

The optimistic case: AI/tech should serve **ambitious learning** that wasn't previously possible, while **protecting teacher agency**. The wrong path is using AI to make a compromised model of schooling more efficient.

## Strongly-felt threads

### 1. Teacher agency is being stripped
Scripted curricula and tools-as-replacement are eroding the conditions for great teaching. Any AI-in-education vision that further reduces teacher judgment is a category error.

### 2. Ambition over complacency
Schools settled into their current shape out of historical constraint, not because that shape is what learning needs. New technology should let us chase what was previously impossible, not optimize the compromise.

### 3. Augmentation, not efficiency
The framing battle. "Efficiency" in education usually means doing the existing thing faster, which entrenches it. "Augmentation" means enabling kinds of learning that couldn't happen without the tool. Most edtech sits on the wrong side of this line.

## What I don't yet know

- How these threads connect into one argument vs. three.
- Whether "ambitious learning" is best defined positively (transfer, agency, creation, motivation) or by contrast with current practice.
- Which empirical claims I'm leaning on without yet having checked.
- Where the literature complicates this frame in ways I haven't priced in.

## For the agent

When you find research that **complicates** any of the above, that's higher signal than research that confirms it. Surface complications first in each digest.
