## The clarified thesis

The medium of learning has been changing throughout history. AI is the
latest, most consequential change to that medium. In an unfettered
environment — with teachers equipped with AI and freed from the system's
workload constraints — we can pursue the truest forms of learning,
whatever the research says those forms actually are. If the research says
projects, great. If the research says structured direct instruction for
novices, also great. The optimistic case is not about a particular
pedagogy. It is about removing the constraints that have forced education
to compromise on its aim.

AI specifically removes four constraints:

1. **The cost of expert attention.** 1:1 tutoring used to require an
   expensive human; AI provides it at near-zero marginal cost.
2. **The one-way nature of fixed content.** Textbooks, videos, lectures
   don't adapt. AI is conversational; it can re-explain and meet the
   learner where they are.
3. **The system-imposed workload that forces teachers to teach to the
   middle of 30.** This is a constraint on the *system*, not a deficiency
   in teachers. AI's role here is to give teachers back the time and
   personalization-bandwidth the system has been taking from them.
4. **The gap between students and expert-level practice.** Students were
   limited to textbook problems; AI can generate domain-authentic tasks,
   simulate expert reasoning, and provide expert-level feedback.

## Three framings the agent must not use

These have appeared in prior writing and need to be corrected:

1. **Never frame teachers as a bottleneck.** Teachers are not the
   constraint. The system is. AI's value to a teacher is not that it
   routes around them; it is that it removes the workload constraints
   that have made what they want to do impossible. When you encounter
   "teacher as bottleneck" language in the literature, surface it as a
   framing the thesis explicitly rejects.

2. **The augmentation-vs-efficiency binary is too loose to do load-bearing
   work.** AI is method-agnostic in service of the aim. KSC vs. Papert is
   a debate *within* the augmentation aim about how to scaffold novices,
   not against it. The thread file can keep its name, but the binary it
   was originally built on is softer than v1 implied.

3. **Tech-cycles is setup, not foil.** The historical wave-by-wave pattern
   is the *frame* — the medium of learning has been changing all along,
   AI is the next change. It is **not** the *obstacle* — "every prior wave
   failed so this one has to prove itself." When Cuban / Watters / Postman
   land, read them as historical setup for "the medium evolves," not as a
   base rate the optimistic case has to beat.

## Direction for the next 3 weeks

### Evidence balance: run a supporting-sprint

The protocol's steelman-first discipline has produced a ledger heavily
weighted toward complication (KSC and Jordan are the two anchors so far).
That's correct ledger hygiene, but the optimistic case has not yet been
read at full strength. For the next three weeks, prioritise the strongest
*supporting* literature.

Three target works for the sprint, in priority order:

1. **Scardamalia & Bereiter on Knowledge Building / CSILE / Knowledge
   Forum.** The most rigorous program of research on a kind of learning
   that the technology actually made possible. Tests the augmentation
   claim with structured, scaffolded inquiry that does not collapse into
   the discovery-pedagogy KSC attack. Specific entry-point candidates:
   the *Cambridge Handbook of the Learning Sciences* chapter (Scardamalia
   & Bereiter, 2006/2014); the foundational CSILE papers.

2. **VanLehn (2011) "The Relative Effectiveness of Human Tutoring,
   Intelligent Tutoring Systems, and Other Tutoring Systems"**,
   *Educational Psychologist*, 46(4). The contemporary update to Bloom's
   2-sigma. Tests the "cost of expert attention" constraint directly:
   what is the actual effect size of ITS / 1:1 / group instruction, and
   how does it bear on the AI-tutoring case?

3. **The Deeper Learning Network evaluation reports** (American Institutes
   for Research, c.2014). The largest contemporary evaluation of schools
   built around ambitious learning. Tests the supporting case at scale.

Continue to surface counter-evidence honestly when it appears in these
sources — the steer is not "go find supporting quotes." It is "read the
strongest supporting literature with the same discipline you've applied
to the steelmen."

### Thread topology

The four threads are not strictly nested, but they do have different
roles in the essay:

- **Ambition over complacency** — the *aim*. What is education for. The
  positive claim.
- **Augmentation, not efficiency** — the *means debate*. How AI serves
  the aim. Currently the most contested thread; benefits most from the
  supporting sprint.
- **Teacher agency** — the *condition*. The workload-constraint
  reframing is load-bearing here. AI is in service of teachers, not
  routed around them.
- **Tech-cycles in education** — the *historical frame*. The medium of
  learning has been changing all along; AI is the latest change. Setup,
  not foil.

This is a hypothesis, not a settled topology. If the supporting sprint
surfaces a better structure, surface it in the 2026-06-13 digest's
thesis-fit check.
