# Thread: Teacher agency

Thesis-seed v1: *"Scripted curricula and tools-as-replacement are eroding the conditions for great teaching. Any AI-in-education vision that further reduces teacher judgment is a category error."*

**Status (2026-05-12, second pass):** First verified claims entered. Dewey 1897 and Illich 1971 are in as visionary anchors. Lemov 2010, Direct Instruction tradition, and Apple / Au on de-skilling are still pending — Lemov is the most important next read because it is the strongest practitioner counter to the seed claim.

---

## Claim: The teacher's vocation is to select and arrange community influences, not to deliver predetermined content — and reforms that strip this judgment role are a category error.

This is the visionary-layer formulation of the thesis seed. Stated as a claim about what *good* teaching is, not yet as an empirical claim about what current reforms do. Empirical layer pending.

### Supporting evidence
- "I believe that the teacher is not in the school to impose certain ideas or to form certain habits in the child, but is there as a member of the community to select the influences which shall affect the child and to assist him in properly responding to these influences." — [Dewey 1897](../sources/Dewey-1897-pedagogic-creed.md), ¶20 — layer: visionary
- "I believe that under existing conditions far too much of the stimulus and control proceeds from the teacher, because of neglect of the idea of the school as a form of social life." — [Dewey 1897](../sources/Dewey-1897-pedagogic-creed.md), ¶19 — layer: visionary. (Dewey's framing is interesting: too much *teacher-as-controller* is itself a failure of teacher agency in the richer sense — agency to set conditions rather than to enforce them.)
- "Skill teachers are made scarce by the belief in the value of licenses. Certification constitutes a form of market manipulation and is plausible only to a schooled mind." — [Illich 1971](../sources/Illich-1971-deschooling-society.md), Ch. 1, p. 8 — layer: visionary. (Illich's distinct contribution: the credentialing regime is itself the mechanism that strips teacher agency. Reforms that work *within* the regime cannot restore agency; they can only redistribute it among the credentialed.)
- "I believe that every teacher should realize the dignity of his calling; that he is a social servant set apart for the maintenance of proper social order and the securing of the right social growth." — [Dewey 1897](../sources/Dewey-1897-pedagogic-creed.md), ¶72 — layer: visionary

### Counter-evidence
- **Pending.** The strongest counter is the empirical case for scripted curricula raising the floor for under-prepared teachers (Lemov 2010, Direct Instruction tradition, Reading First era). The thread cannot close without it. Until then, Dewey and Illich's position is supported but not contested in this thread.
- A partial internal counter from Illich himself: "the strongly motivated student who is faced with the task of acquiring a new and complex skill may benefit greatly from the discipline now associated with the old-fashioned schoolmaster who taught reading, Hebrew, catechism, or multiplication by rote" ([Illich 1971](../sources/Illich-1971-deschooling-society.md), Ch. 1, p. 7-8) — Illich concedes that skill-drill teaching can be valuable; his objection is to compulsion and certification, not to skilled instruction. The agency-vs-script frame may be sharper than Illich's own argument supports.

### Open questions
- What is the empirically strongest evidence that scripted curricula raise the floor for under-prepared teachers (Lemov, Direct Instruction tradition, Reading First era), and does that floor come at a ceiling cost?
- Is "teacher agency" one construct or three — autonomy over content, over method, over pacing? The literatures on each may not behave the same.
- Where is the cleanest empirical work on de-skilling — Apple, Au, others? Does it survive replication or stay in the qualitative-theory tradition?
- Distinguish "teacher agency" from "teacher discretion to do harm." The strongest counter to the seed is that some agency-removing reforms (e.g., scripted high-fidelity phonics) produce real gains.
