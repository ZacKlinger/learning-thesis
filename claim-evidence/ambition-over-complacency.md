# Thread: Ambition over complacency

Thesis-seed v1: *"Schools settled into their current shape out of historical constraint, not because that shape is what learning needs. New technology should let us chase what was previously impossible, not optimize the compromise."*

**Status (2026-05-23):** First entries land this Saturday. Dewey 1897 provides
the foundational visionary articulation of education as a "process of living
and not a preparation for future living" — a description that frames the
compromise the seed names. Hirsch (1987) and the Tyack & Cuban historical
record remain the most important counters and have not yet been read. The
thread is **not** yet doing serious work; it is opening a frame that needs
several more sessions of reading to fill in.

---

## Claim: A defensible aim for education is to make the school a *form of living* — present, intrinsically valuable, continuous with the child's experience — rather than a preparation for an unknown future.

This is Dewey's frame, not yet a measurable claim. Its evidentiary status
is *visionary aim-setting*. The empirical question "does education organised
this way produce measurable learning gains" is a different question, and
Mehta & Fine (2019) is the most relevant practitioner-empirical entry to
fill that in next.

### Supporting evidence
- "I believe that education, therefore, is a process of living and not a
  preparation for future living."
  — [Dewey 1897](../sources/Dewey-1897-pedagogic-creed.md), Article Two, ¶8 — layer: visionary
- "I believe that education which does not occur through forms of life, forms
  that are worth living for their own sake, is always a poor substitute for
  the genuine reality and tends to cramp and to deaden."
  — [Dewey 1897](../sources/Dewey-1897-pedagogic-creed.md), Article Two, ¶10 — layer: visionary
- "I believe that much of present education fails because it neglects this
  fundamental principle of the school as a form of community life. It
  conceives the school as a place where certain information is to be given,
  where certain lessons are to be learned, or where certain habits are to be
  formed. The value of these is conceived as lying largely in the remote
  future... As a result they do not become a part of the life experience of
  the child and so are not truly educative."
  — [Dewey 1897](../sources/Dewey-1897-pedagogic-creed.md), Article Two, ¶16 — layer: visionary
- "I believe finally, that education must be conceived as a continuing
  reconstruction of experience; that the process and the goal of education are
  one and the same thing."
  — [Dewey 1897](../sources/Dewey-1897-pedagogic-creed.md), Article Three, ¶40 — layer: visionary

### Counter-evidence (against the thesis seed)
- *(none yet read in the verified sense — but the two strongest counters that
  must enter before the thread can do heavy work are:*
  - *Hirsch 1987 (Cultural Literacy / Core Knowledge): "ambition" without a
    content theory leaves disadvantaged students without the knowledge floor
    they need. The most coherent insistence on shared content as the
    precondition for ambition, not its opposite.*
  - *The actual founding-document record (Committee of Ten 1893, Cardinal
    Principles 1918, Conant 1959) on which the "schools were designed for
    factories" rhetoric mostly does not survive contact. Tyack & Cuban's
    "Tinkering Toward Utopia" is the right corrective; not yet read.)*

### Open questions
- The historical-constraint argument is often asserted, rarely sourced. The
  rhetorical version (Prussian/factory-model) is mostly mythology; the actual
  founding documents pushed in *different* directions and a thread that
  invokes "the current shape of school" should name which shape, when, by
  whom. (Restated from v1.)
- Sizer's *Horace's Compromise* and Mehta & Fine's *In Search of Deeper
  Learning* both document how rare ambition is in practice — including in
  schools that explicitly aim for it. Why? Pedagogical? Structural?
  Adult-labour-conditions? (Restated from v1.)
- Dewey's "process of living" is a defensible aim. Whether the schools that
  best instantiate it produce comparable test-score evidence to those that do
  not is a separate, empirical question. The thread should not let the aim
  become evidence for itself.
- Distinct from the seed: "previously impossible learning" needs an
  operational definition, or "ambition" collapses into "things I find more
  exciting than the current curriculum." (Carried forward from
  augmentation-not-efficiency.)
